NIXI Auction Portal

Business flow manual (non-technical)

This document describes end-to-end processes for registration, bidding, payments, and administration. It is written for stakeholders who need a clear operational picture without reference to software implementation.

1. Roles and participants

  • Applicants and bidders register on the platform, verify their identity, join auctions where permitted, place bids, and complete payments when they win or when fees apply.
  • Platform administrators configure and manage auctions, oversee users, enforce policy (including blocking accounts when required), communicate with users, and review activity for oversight.

2. New user registration

The registration path establishes a verified identity before an applicant may participate financially.

  1. The applicant begins registration and provides contact details.
  2. Email is confirmed using a one-time code sent to that address.
  3. Mobile number is confirmed using a one-time code sent by SMS.
  4. PAN is verified through an authorised identity check.
  5. The system ensures the applicant is not disallowed under the organisation’s block list rules (aligned email, phone, identity, device, or network indicators as applicable).
  6. The applicant pays the registration fee through the integrated payment channel.
  7. After successful payment, the account is activated. Where policy requires, the user may need to complete password setup before full access.
Business rules. Verification codes expire after a short window (typically about ten minutes). Duplicate email or mobile registration is not permitted. Individuals who match block-list criteria cannot complete registration.

3. Login and password recovery

Login

Users sign in with email and password. Accounts that are blocked or otherwise restricted under policy cannot proceed until resolved through support or administrative action.

Forgotten password

The user requests a one-time code sent to the registered email address, confirms the code within the allowed time, and then chooses a new password.

4. Auction lifecycle

Auctions progress through operational stages such as:

  • Not yet open — scheduled or visible but not yet accepting bids under the stated rules.
  • Live — open for participation (if a participation fee applies) and for bidding according to published rules.
  • Closed or ended — bidding stops; outcome and settlement steps apply.
  • Cancelled — the auction does not conclude under the normal sale path.

Administrators create and maintain auctions and may close, reopen, or cancel them in line with operational policy.

5. Bidder journey

  1. The user logs in and selects an auction.
  2. If business rules require a participation fee for that auction, the user pays it once through the payment channel. After payment succeeds, they are recognised as eligible to bid on that auction.
  3. The user places bids. Each bid must meet the minimum next bid requirement: the greater of the current leading offer and the starting price, plus the stated minimum increment.
  4. Near the scheduled end time, anti-sniping rules may extend the end time so that late bids can be responded to, depending on platform configuration.
  5. Users may track auctions of interest and review their own bid history and recent auction activity.
Fair use. Only users who have satisfied participation requirements (when they exist) may bid while the auction is live. Rapid repeated bidding may be limited to reduce misuse.

6. Winner and payment after closure

  1. When bidding ends and the auction is closed, a winner is determined according to published rules (typically the highest qualifying bid).
  2. The winner must pay the amount due (as defined by policy for that auction) within a defined payment window.
  3. When payment is received and confirmed, the transaction is treated as settled for that winner.

If the winner does not pay on time

  • The platform may record a default against the user.
  • After repeated defaults, policy may allow account restriction or blocking and inclusion on the block list.
  • The opportunity may pass to the next eligible bidder (for example, second or third rank), with a new payment window, according to organisational rules.
  • A scheduled operational process identifies expired payment windows and applies promotion and default handling in line with policy.

7. Invoices and records

Users and authorised administrators can obtain invoices or payment records for registration fees and auction-related payments, supporting finance, tax, and audit needs.

8. Profile and identity updates

Logged-in users may update sensitive details such as password, email, or mobile number. Changes that affect identity use one-time confirmation (email or SMS) and the same block-list checks used at registration, so policy cannot be bypassed by changing contact data.

9. Communication and support

  • Administrators may send messages to selected users; conversations can continue in structured threads with replies.
  • Users can reach support through the channels enabled for the deployment (for example, tickets or the same messaging path).

10. Compliance and audit

  • Block list — activity is blocked when indicators match blocked records under policy.
  • Audit trail — important actions are recorded for review by authorised staff.
  • Bid and outcome records — retained to support transparency and dispute resolution.

11. End-to-end overview

This manual describes business processes only. Operational cut-off times, fee amounts, payment windows, and enforcement thresholds follow organisational policy and published auction terms.
